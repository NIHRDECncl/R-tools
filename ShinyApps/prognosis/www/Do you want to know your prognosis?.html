Asking the time frame would do my head in

Getting a prognosis can be distressing, and is often neither wanted nor needed.   

So, if you are worried about what you might find here, and don't need to know about your (or a loved one's) prognosis, close the app --- but, before you go, please remember that if you are ever given a number, there is so much uncertainty around it (i.e. the time frame is so wide) that any single number has a 50% chance of being wildly wrong (as proved by Annie).

However, many people do want to understand what a prognosis means, patients should know how to ask their doctors the "right" questions about prognosis, and doctors and other health care professionals need to be able to interpret the numbers for their patients and patients' families.

If you are comfortable with graphs, you can use this app to find out:
  • What a prognosis is, and why it is not just "news you don't want to get"
  • Why a single number is misleading --- never ever take at face value statements such as "your prognosis is x% over n weeks/months/years"
  • That a prognosis changes with new information --- the most obvious example of such new information would be the fact that you have survived for so many weeks/months/years
  • That a prognosis is very uncertain --- so you need to know what the chances are for:
    • the best outcome you can hope for
    • the worst outcome you might consider planning for
    • the average outcome in a group of people similar to you, and how precise the estimate is

If you are not comfortable with graphs, you could give the app a try and see if it works for you. Or, you could wait a while until we have wordy explanations to go with the graphs.

If the condition you are interested in is not in here, you might want to see what the general principles are for understanding any prognosis.
