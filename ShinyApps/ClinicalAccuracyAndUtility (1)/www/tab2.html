How to read the diagnostic accuracy table and graph 0: Test accuracy

The figure below is a screen snip showing how the table and graph 0 look when the app is first started, with no customisation of the labels or accuracy data.


Figure. Screensnip of the diagnostic accuracy table and graph 0.

Look at the table

The table compares the results of the test with the condition being tested for.

  1. If the test is positive ("tested +ve" row), there are two possibilities: the result is accurate, a true positive; or it is inaccurate, a false positive --- TP and FP.
  2. If the test is negative ("tested -ve" row), there are two possibilities: the result is inaccurate, a false negative; or it is accurate, a true negative --- FN and TN.

The table shows the numbers of true and false postives (TP, FP) and the numbers of fales and true negatives (FN, TN)

Look at the 2 charts in graph 0

The graph shows the data in the table in two ways: for the numbers and for the proportions of TP, FP, FN, and TN. Numbers are shown on the left chart, and proportions on the right.

Each chart has 3 columns.
  1. The left hand columns of the two charts show the numbers and proportions of (reading from the bottom up) TP, FN, FP, TN.
    Notice that the bottom two sections, TP and FN, together represent the prevalence of the condition in the tested population.
  2. The middle columns show the people who tested positive: TP and FP.
    The middle column on the right hand chart shows the true positive and false postive rates.
  3. The right hand columns show the people who tested negative: TN and FN.
    The right hand column on the right hand chart shows the true and false negative rates.

Customise the app

Click on the tab to "Explore clinical accuracy and utility of diagnostic tests".

Use the form on the left to:

  • Change the labels for population, test, and condition
    (and click on the update button --- it is slow, so please be patient; we are working on improving the response time)

    Do the table and graphs show the names of the population, test, and condition you are interested in?

  • Change the prevalence (and click on the update button)
    How do the TP, FP, FN, TN change on the table and charts?
    (Don't worry about graphs 1 and 2 just yet!)

  • Change the sensitivity (and click on the update button)
    How do the TP, FP, FN, TN change on the table and charts?
    Why? (Hint: sensitivity = (number of true postives / number with the condition))

  • Change the specificity (and click on the update button)
    How do the TP, FP, FN, TN change on the table and charts?
    Why? (Hint: specificity = (number of true negatives / number without the condition))

  • Change the number of people in the study that provided the data: from 300 to 30 and then to 3000.
    How do the TP, FP, FN, TN change on the table and charts?
