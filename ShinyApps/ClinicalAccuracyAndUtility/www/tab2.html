Reading graph 1:Assessing clinical accuracy 

Graph 1 illustrates the clinically important measures of the accuracy of a diagnostic test: i.e. the post-test probabilities.


Figure. Screensnip of graph 1.

The X-axis shows the possible range of pretest probabilities: from 0 to 1.

The Y axis shows  the the possible range of post-test probabilities: from 0 to 1

Ribbons (the shaded area around lines) show 95% confidence intervals.

Line 1, with the salmon pink ribbon, shows the relation between pre-test probability and post-postive test probability. 
Note that the  post-positive test probability.equals the positive predictive value.

Line 5, also with a salmon pink ribbon, shows the relation between the possible prevalance of the condition and the post-postive test probability.

Line 2, with the sea green ribbon, shows the relation between pre-test probability and post-negative test probability.
Note that the  post-negative test probability.equals 1 minus the negative predictive value.

Line 4, also with a sea green ribbon, shows the relation between the possible prevalance of the condition and the post-negative test probability.

Line 3 shows the actual pre-test probability.
Follow the line upwards to where it crosses the two pre-to-post-test probablity curves.
The lines drawn leftwards to the Y-axis show the post-negative and post-postive test probabilities.
