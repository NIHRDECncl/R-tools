Look at the graph

The X-axis shows the possible range of pretest probabilities: from 0 to 1.

The Y axis shows  the the possible range of post-test probabilities: from 0 to 1

Ribbons (the shaded area around lines) show 95% confidence intervals. These represent statistical uncertainty, or lack of precision, in measurment.

The upper curved line, with the salmon pink ribbon and the lower curved line, with the sea green ribbon show how test results change the probability of having the condition. In the example shown, the prevalence = 40%, sensitivity = 90%, and specificity = 80%, and:

  1. a positive test increases the probability of the condition to 75%.
  2. a negative test decreases the probability of the condition to 8%.
