Welcome to the NIHR DEC Newcastle's tool for assessing the technnical accuracy of a diagnostic test

This Shiny app allows you to explore and discover the factors that determine the technical  measures of the accuracy of a diagnostic test: i.e. sensitivity and specificity.

We use the term technical accuracy  to contrast with clinical accuracy, which is the probability of the condition given that the test result is positive or negative. The measures of clinical accuracy are post-positive test probability andpost-negative test probability ; These are equivalent to positive and negative predictive values (PPV and NPV). These are the measures that patients and clinicians understand intuitively.

You can explore the factors that determine clinical accuracy (and clinical utillity, the net benefit) in a companion app:
Clinical accuracy and clinical utility.

A third companion app allows you to explore the uncertainties in assessing test accuracy when the reference standard is not perfect.

How to use this app

Use the left-hand panel to:

  • enter the data that will be interpreted in the graphs

  • control what is shown in the graphs, i.e. classification and confidence intervals.

Use the tabs at the top (i) to review information about using the app and reading the graphs, and (ii) to view the graphs
